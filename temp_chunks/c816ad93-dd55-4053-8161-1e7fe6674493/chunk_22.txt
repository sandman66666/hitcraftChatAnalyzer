**2. Basic Chord Types:**
- **Major Chords**: Often sound happy or bright (e.g., C, G, D).
- **Minor Chords**: Typically sound sad or introspective (e.g., Am, Em, Dm).
- **Seventh Chords**: Add depth and complexity (e.g., C7, D7).

- **I-V-vi-IV**: Popular in pop music (e.g., C-G-Am-F).
- **ii-V-I**: A common progression in jazz (e.g., Dm-G-C in C major).

- **Choose a Key**: Determine the scale you'll be working in (e.g., C major, A minor).
- **Experiment with Chords**: Start with the I, IV, and V chords, and see how they sound together. Try adding minor chords for variation.
- **Play with Rhythm**: The way you strum or play the chords can change the feel of the progression. Experiment with different rhythms!
