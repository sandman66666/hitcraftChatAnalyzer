### Quick Takeaway
**[TLDR]:** Chord progressions are sequences of chords played in a piece of music that create harmony and emotion. A common progression is I-IV-V-I.

### Understanding Chord Progressions
1. **Basic Definition**: A chord progression is a series of chords played in succession. They can evoke different feelings and are foundational to song structure.

2. **Common Progressions**: Some well-known chord progressions include:
   - **I-IV-V-I**: This is very common in pop and rock music. For example, in C major, the chords would be C (I), F (IV), G (V), and back to C (I).
   - **ii-V-I**: Frequently used in jazz. In C major, this would be Dm (ii), G (V), and C (I).
   - **I-vi-IV-V**: Known as the "50s progression," it's used in many classic hits. In C major, it would be C (I), Am (vi), F (IV), and G (V).

### Examples in Songs
To help illustrate these concepts, consider songs like:
- **"Let It Be" by The Beatles**: This song uses the I-IV-V progression effectively.
