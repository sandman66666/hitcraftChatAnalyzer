3. **Use High-Pass Filters**: These can help remove unnecessary low-end rumble from non-bass instruments. Start around 80Hz for most instruments, but adjust based on the specific sound.
4. **Enhance Presence**: Boosting around 3-5kHz can add clarity and presence to vocals and instruments.
5. **Add Warmth**: A gentle boost around 200-300Hz can add warmth to vocals or instruments, but be careful not to make them muddy.

### Practical Application:
- **Vocals**: Apply a high-pass filter to eliminate low-end rumble, enhance presence with a small boost around 3-5kHz, and consider cutting around 300-500Hz if they sound too boxy.
- **Guitars**: Use a high-pass filter, then boost around 2-4kHz for articulation. Cut around 200-400Hz if the guitar sounds too muddy.
- **Drums**: High-pass the overheads to clean up low-end. Boost the kick around 60-100Hz for punch, and boost the snare around 200Hz for body.
