   A chord progression is a series of chords played in succession. Each chord creates a different mood, and how they connect can evoke various emotions in the listener.

2. **Common Chord Progressions**:  
   Here are a few classic progressions you might find useful:
   - **I-IV-V**: This is one of the most common progressions, especially in rock and pop music. For example, in the key of C major, the chords would be C (I), F (IV), and G (V).

   - **I-vi-IV-V**: This progression is often heard in doo-wop music. In C major, it would be C (I), Am (vi), F (IV), G (V).
